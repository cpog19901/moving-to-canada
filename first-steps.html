First Steps

Apply for a visa

To apply for a two year Canadian Visa its best to visit the CIC website. The visa costs about €200 in total and there are a set amount of applications available each year. When I did my visa in 2016 there were a number of pools to which you would be picked to complete your application. My advice would be to have your passport ready and ensure that it's not expiring any time soon.

Save Money

Make sure you save enough money before you go over to Canada. The minimum that is required by the IEC is around €1200 to have in your account when your first arrive in Canada. Although this is the requirement I would recommend bringing at least €7000. Canada can be a very expensive country and if you plan to treat your first few weeks as a vacation like I did I would certainly consider bringing a large sum of money. When you add up the cost of accomodation,groceries and transport for the first few weeks the cash can empty from your pockets quite quickly.

Plan where you want to go

Canada has so many amazing cities that it can be hard to pick from them. Do some research on each city your consider and weigh things up. Take into account the cost of living and climate in that city. Places like Toronto, Calgary and Montreal can get very cold in the winter and it may not suit everyone. If you choose somewhere like Vancouver the climate it very similar to Ireland. (It rains a lot there too.)

Be Prepared

Make a list of the things you need to do once you get your visa. Book your flights as early as possible as it will be cheaper than booking a few days or weeks before. There are lot of airlines to choose from e.g Air Lingus, Air Canada and Blank. I personally recomment the latter to save you the extra cash. Depending on when you're moving over to Canada, bring the correct gear. If you're going in Summer pack some shorts, shirts and sun cream. If you're going in Winter do you best to buy a winter coat and snow boots before your journey. It will save you the hassle of doing the shopping during winter.
